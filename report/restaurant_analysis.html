## 5. Key Findings

The analysis indicates that total bill amount is the strongest predictor of tipping behavior. As customer spending increases, tips increase significantly.

Group size also shows a positive relationship with tipping, suggesting that larger parties tend to tip more.

No statistically significant differences were observed across days of the week, time of service, or smoking status.

6. Recommendations

  • Focus on increasing total bill value through upselling or bundling strategies
  • Prioritize service strategies for larger groups
  • Avoid overemphasizing operational changes based on day or time

7. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that revenue-driven factors, particularly total bill size, are the primary drivers of tipping behavior. Strategic efforts to increase customer spending are likely to yield the greatest impact.
