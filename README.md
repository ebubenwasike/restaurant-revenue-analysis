# restaurant revenue optimization analysis

## About This Project
I am attempting using statistical methods to solve realworld problems like restaurant revenue optimization. (Originally created in January of 2026)

In this project, I simulate a statistical consulting scenario where a restaurant wants to better understand customer spending and tipping behavior in order to improve revenue at the restaurant.

---

## Objective
The goal of this analysis is to identify the key factors that influence tipping and overall spending and translate these findings into actionable recommendations for business.

---

## My Approach
I approached this project as a consultant by:
- Cleaning and preparing raw data for analysis  
- Exploring relationships between variables  
- Applying statistical methods such as linear regression and ANOVA  
- Interpreting results in a way that is understandable to non-technical stakeholders  

---

## Key Insights
- Total bill amount is the strongest predictor of tipping behavior (obv) 
- Larger groups tend to generate higher tips  
- Day and time of service do not significantly impact tipping  

---

## Recommendations
Based on the analysis, I would recommend:
- Focusing on strategies that increase total bill value like upselling  
- Targeting larger groups to maximize revenue potential  
- Avoiding unnecessary operational changes based on day or time
